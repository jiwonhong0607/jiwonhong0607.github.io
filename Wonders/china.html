Wulingyuan is a region in China known for its mountainous landscape.
Located north of Zhangjiajie City, it is one of the most popular places to visit in China as it is considered a national heritage.
Covering an area of 690 square kilometers (266 square miles), Wulingyuan Scenic Area consists of four parts- Zhangjiajie National Forest Park, Suoxi Valley Nature Reserve, Tianzi Mountain Nature Reserve and the newly added Yangjiajie Scenic Area.
The Wulingyuan mountains were formed over 400-million years ago through a series of unique geological events leaving behind one of the most unique rock formations on Earth.
