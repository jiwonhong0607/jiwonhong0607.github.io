Lake Baikal is a fresh water lake in Russia.
It is the largest freshwater lake in the world (by volume) and the world's deepest lake.
In 1996 it was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
The volume of the lake is 5,521 cubic miles of water (23,013 cubic kilometers). This volume is approximately equivalent to all five of the North American Great Lakes combined
The depth is 5,354 feet (1,632 meters). Its lowest point lies more than 4,000 feet (1,219 m) below sea level.
This makes this lake, the largest fresh water lake in the world.
