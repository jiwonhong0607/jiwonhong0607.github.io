Lapland is a forest stretching across northern Norway, Sweden, and Finland and into the Kola Peninsula of Russia.
It is bounded by the Norwegian Sea on the west, the Barents Sea on the north, and the White Sea on the east.
The word Lapland is derived from Lapp, the name Scandinavians ascribed to the Sami people, who have sparsely inhabited the region for several thousand years.
